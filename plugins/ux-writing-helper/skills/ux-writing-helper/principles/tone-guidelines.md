# Tone Guidelines

Tone is how your words soundâ€”the emotional quality and personality behind your message. The right tone makes users feel understood, supported, and confident. This guide helps you choose the appropriate tone for different contexts.

## The Three Core Tones

### 1. Friendly & Approachable

**When to use**:
- Onboarding new users
- Welcome messages and getting started flows
- Success states and celebrations
- Marketing and landing pages
- Community features (comments, sharing)
- Non-critical help content

**Characteristics**:
- Uses contractions ("We're", "You'll", "Don't")
- Conversational language (like talking to a friend)
- Personal pronouns ("we", "you")
- Positive, encouraging language
- Occasional warmth (without being unprofessional)
- Can include appropriate emoji in some contexts

**Examples**:

| Context | Friendly Tone |
|---------|--------------|
| Welcome message | "Welcome! We're excited to have you here. Let's set up your accountâ€”it'll only take a minute." |
| Success state | "You're all set! Your profile looks great." |
| Empty state | "Nothing here yet! Click 'Add Item' to get started." |
| Encouragement | "Almost there! Just one more step." |
| Getting started | "Let's build your first project together." |

**When to avoid**: Errors, security contexts, legal notices, high-stakes actions

---

### 2. Professional & Formal

**When to use**:
- Legal notices and terms of service
- Security and privacy settings
- Account deletion or other permanent actions
- Billing and payment contexts
- Enterprise products
- Compliance and regulatory content
- Formal business communications

**Characteristics**:
- Complete sentences, proper grammar
- Minimal or no contractions
- Formal, precise language
- Objective, authoritative tone
- Clear, unambiguous wording
- No slang or casual expressions

**Examples**:

| Context | Professional Tone |
|---------|------------------|
| Legal notice | "By continuing, you agree to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy." |
| Security action | "Two-factor authentication has been enabled for your account." |
| Account deletion | "This action is permanent and cannot be undone. All your data will be deleted within 30 days." |
| Payment confirmation | "Your payment of $99.99 has been processed successfully." |
| Enterprise feature | "Contact your administrator to request access to this feature." |

**When to avoid**: Casual contexts, onboarding, celebration moments

---

### 3. Helpful & Supportive

**When to use**:
- Error messages
- Troubleshooting help
- Form validation feedback
- Support and help documentation
- "How to" guides and tutorials
- Situations where users might be confused or frustrated

**Characteristics**:
- Empathetic and understanding
- Solution-focused, not problem-focused
- Explains what happened and why
- Always provides next steps
- Calm and reassuring
- Acknowledges user feelings without being patronizing

**Examples**:

| Context | Helpful Tone |
|---------|-------------|
| Form error | "This email format isn't quite right. Please use a format like name@example.com" |
| Network error | "We couldn't connect to the server. Please check your internet connection and try again." |
| Permission error | "You'll need admin permissions to do this. Contact your team owner if you need access." |
| Lost work warning | "You have unsaved changes. Save now or they'll be lost." |
| System error | "Something went wrong on our end. We're looking into itâ€”please try again in a few minutes." |

**When to avoid**: Marketing content, casual conversations

## Tone Combinations

You often need to blend tones. Here's how to mix them effectively:

### Friendly + Helpful
**Use for**: Helpful content in consumer products

"Looks like that page doesn't exist. Let's get you back on trackâ€”try searching or visit the homepage."

### Professional + Helpful
**Use for**: Enterprise errors, security issues

"Your session has expired for security reasons. Please sign in again to continue."

### Friendly + Professional
**Use for**: B2B onboarding, professional tools

"Welcome to the team! We've created your workspace and you're ready to start collaborating."

## Tone by Context

### Onboarding & First-Time Use
**Tone**: Friendly & Helpful

**Goals**: Make users feel welcome, reduce anxiety, encourage exploration

**Examples**:
- "Welcome! Let's personalize your experience. This will take about 2 minutes."
- "Great choice! Now let's set up your profile."
- "You're doing greatâ€”just one more step!"

**Avoid**:
- Being too formal: "Commence user initialization procedure"
- Being too casual: "Yo! Let's get this party started!"

---

### Success States & Confirmations
**Tone**: Friendly & Positive (but brief)

**Goals**: Celebrate user achievements, provide confidence

**Examples**:
- "Success! Your changes have been saved."
- "Welcome aboard! Your account is ready."
- "All done. We've sent you a confirmation email."
- "Perfect! Your photo has been uploaded."

**Avoid**:
- Over-celebrating: "OMG YESSS!!! YOU DID IT!!!"
- Being too dry: "Operation completed."

---

### Error Messages
**Tone**: Helpful & Calm (with empathy)

**Goals**: Reduce frustration, provide clear solutions, maintain trust

**Structure**: [What happened] + [Why] + [What to do]

**Examples**:
- "We couldn't save your changes. Your internet connection was lost. Please reconnect and try again."
- "This password is too common. Choose a more unique password to keep your account secure."
- "That link has expired. For security, links expire after 24 hours. Request a new one below."

**Avoid**:
- Blame: "You entered the wrong password"
- Robotic: "Error 404: Resource not found"
- Overly casual: "Oops! Something broke ðŸ˜…"

---

### Warnings & Destructive Actions
**Tone**: Professional & Clear (with appropriate gravity)

**Goals**: Ensure users understand consequences, prevent mistakes

**Examples**:
- "Delete this project? This action cannot be undone. All files will be permanently deleted."
- "You're about to remove 3 team members. They'll lose access to all project files immediately."
- "Changing this setting will log you out of all devices for security."

**Avoid**:
- Being too casual: "Sure you wanna delete this?"
- Being alarming: "WARNING! DANGER! DELETE OPERATION!"
- Being vague: "Are you sure?"

---

### Empty States
**Tone**: Friendly & Encouraging

**Goals**: Explain why it's empty, encourage first action

**Examples**:
- "No projects yet. Create your first project to get started."
- "Your inbox is emptyâ€”you're all caught up!"
- "You haven't added any team members. Invite your team to start collaborating."

**Avoid**:
- Negative framing: "You have nothing here"
- Too robotic: "No items found"

---

### Help & Educational Content
**Tone**: Helpful & Clear

**Goals**: Teach without patronizing, enable self-service

**Examples**:
- "To invite team members, click 'Share' in the top right, then enter their email addresses."
- "Your password must be at least 8 characters and include a number. This keeps your account secure."
- "Can't find what you're looking for? Try using keywords or filter by date."

**Avoid**:
- Patronizing: "This is very simple, even a child could do it"
- Too technical: "Utilize the collaborative interface paradigm"

---

### Loading States & Wait Times
**Tone**: Calm & Informative

**Goals**: Set expectations, reduce anxiety during waits

**Examples**:
- "Uploading your file... This may take a minute."
- "Generating your report... Hang tight!"
- "Processing payment... Please don't close this window."

**Avoid**:
- Vague: "Loading..."
- Overly chatty: "We're working super duper hard on this!"

## Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** is your brand's consistent personality.
**Tone** is how that voice changes based on context.

Think of voice as a person's personality, and tone as their mood in different situations:

- **Voice**: "We're friendly and approachable"
  - **Tone in onboarding**: "Welcome! Let's get started."
  - **Tone in error**: "We couldn't process that. Let's try again."
  - **Tone in deletion**: "This will permanently delete your account."

The voice stays friendly, but the tone adjusts from excited to helpful to serious.

## Cultural Considerations

Tone can be interpreted differently across cultures:

### Humor
- **Use cautiously**: Humor doesn't always translate
- **Safe**: Gentle, universal humor
- **Risky**: Sarcasm, cultural references, wordplay

### Formality
- Some cultures expect more formal language
- "Please" and "thank you" may be required in some markets
- First names vs. last names vary by culture

### Directness
- Western cultures often prefer direct communication
- Some Asian cultures prefer more indirect language
- Adjust based on your audience

## Tone Testing Checklist

When reviewing your copy, ask:

**Is the tone appropriate?**
- [ ] Does it match the context (onboarding, error, success)?
- [ ] Does it match the severity (low stakes vs. high stakes)?
- [ ] Would I use this tone speaking to someone in person?

**Is it empathetic?**
- [ ] Does it acknowledge how the user might feel?
- [ ] Does it avoid blame or making users feel stupid?
- [ ] Does it show you care about their experience?

**Is it helpful?**
- [ ] Does it provide value (information, guidance, reassurance)?
- [ ] Does it tell users what to do next?
- [ ] Does it reduce anxiety or confusion?

**Is it on-brand?**
- [ ] Does it sound like "us"?
- [ ] Does it match our voice guidelines?
- [ ] Would our target audience connect with this?

## Examples: Same Message, Different Tones

**Context**: User's password is incorrect

**Friendly**: "That password doesn't match. Give it another try or reset your password below."

**Professional**: "The password you entered is incorrect. Please try again or use the password reset option."

**Helpful**: "We couldn't sign you in because your password is incorrect. Double-check your password or click 'Forgot password' to reset it."

**Too robotic** (avoid): "Authentication failed. Error code: AUTH_001"

**Too formal** (avoid): "The credentials you have provided do not match our records. Please re-enter your credentials or initiate the password recovery process."

## Summary

Choose your tone based on:
1. **Context**: What's the user doing? (onboarding, encountering error, deleting data)
2. **Emotion**: How might they feel? (excited, confused, anxious)
3. **Stakes**: How serious is the situation? (low stakes = friendly, high stakes = professional)
4. **Brand**: Does this match your voice?

When in doubt, err on the side of helpful and clear over clever and casual.
