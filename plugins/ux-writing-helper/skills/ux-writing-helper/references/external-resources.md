# External UX Writing Resources

Curated list of authoritative style guides, articles, books, tools, and communities for UX writing and content design.

## Official Style Guides

### Google Material Design Writing Guide
**URL**: https://m3.material.io/foundations/content-design

**What it covers**:
- Writing for Material Design (Android/Web)
- Voice and tone principles
- UI text guidelines
- Accessibility and localization

**Why it's valuable**:
- Comprehensive and well-documented
- Examples for every guideline
- Used by thousands of products worldwide
- Regular updates with modern best practices

**Best for**: Mobile and web apps, especially Android

---

### Apple Human Interface Guidelines - Writing
**URL**: https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/writing

**What it covers**:
- Writing for iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS
- Voice and tone
- Terminology and punctuation
- Accessibility
- Localization

**Why it's valuable**:
- Authoritative for Apple platforms
- Strong accessibility focus
- Detailed platform-specific guidance
- Clear examples and rationale

**Best for**: iOS and macOS apps

---

## Books

### "Strategic Writing for UX" by Torrey Podmajersky
**Focus**: Comprehensive guide to UX writing strategy

**Key topics**:
- Voice and tone development
- Content strategy for product teams
- Writing for different UI components
- Collaboration with designers and developers

**Why read it**: Industry-standard reference, practical frameworks

---

### "Microcopy: The Complete Guide" by Kinneret Yifrah
**Focus**: Writing UI text and microcopy

**Key topics**:
- Buttons, labels, and CTAs
- Error messages and confirmations
- Onboarding flows
- Forms and validation

**Why read it**: Comprehensive, lots of examples, very practical

---

### "Conversational Design" by Erika Hall
**Focus**: Conversation principles for UX

**Key topics**:
- Conversational UI patterns
- Chatbots and voice interfaces
- Human-centered language
- Tone and personality

**Why read it**: Essential for conversational interfaces, broader UX writing principles

---

### "Don't Make Me Think" by Steve Krug

**Key topics**:
- Clear navigation labels
- Scannable content
- User-focused language
- Reducing cognitive load

**Why read it**: Usability classic, foundational principles still apply

---

### "The Elements of Content Strategy" by Erin Kissane

## Tools

### Writing & Readability

**Hemingway Editor**
- **URL**: https://hemingwayapp.com/
- **Purpose**: Check reading level, identify complex sentences
- **Free version**: Yes (web)
- **Best for**: Simplifying text, checking readability

**Grammarly**
- **URL**: https://www.grammarly.com/
- **Purpose**: Grammar, spelling, tone suggestions
- **Free version**: Yes (basic)
- **Best for**: Grammar checking, tone adjustment

**Readable**
- **URL**: https://readable.com/
- **Purpose**: Readability scores, reading level
- **Free version**: Limited
- **Best for**: Detailed readability analysis

---

### Accessibility

**WAVE (Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool)**
- **URL**: https://wave.webaim.org/
- **Purpose**: Check web accessibility
- **Free version**: Yes
- **Best for**: Identifying accessibility issues in UI text

**WebAIM Contrast Checker**
- **URL**: https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/
- **Purpose**: Check color contrast ratios
- **Free version**: Yes
- **Best for**: Ensuring text meets WCAG standards

---

## How to Use These Resources

### For Beginners:
1. Start with **Google Material Design Writing Guide** (comprehensive, accessible)
2. Read **"Microcopy: The Complete Guide"** (practical, example-rich)
3. Join **UX Writing Hub** community (ask questions, get feedback)
4. Take **Daily UX Writing Challenge** (build portfolio)

### For Practitioners:
1. Keep **Microsoft Style Guide** or **Apple HIG** bookmarked (reference)
2. Read **Nielsen Norman Group** articles (research-backed insights)
3. Use **Hemingway Editor** and **Grammarly** (daily tools)
4. Follow **UX Collective** on Medium (stay current)

### For Accessibility Focus:
1. Study **WebAIM** resources
2. Read **WCAG Quick Reference**
3. Follow **Gov.UK Design System** (gold standard for accessibility)
4. Join **A11Y Project** community

### For Enterprise/B2B:
1. Study **Microsoft Style Guide** and **Salesforce Lightning**
2. Read **IBM Carbon** content guidelines
3. Focus on **clarity and professionalism** over casual tone
