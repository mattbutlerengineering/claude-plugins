# Onboarding & Help Content Patterns

Onboarding is a user's first impression of your product. Great onboarding copy is welcoming, sets expectations, and guides users to their first success.

## Welcome Messages

### First-Time Welcome

**Pattern**: Welcome + value proposition + set expectations

**Examples**:
- "Welcome! Let's set up your accountâ€”it takes about 2 minutes."
- "Welcome to [Product]! We'll help you get started in just a few steps."
- "Hi! Let's personalize your experience. This will take less than a minute."

**Why it works**:
- Friendly greeting
- Sets time expectations
- Reduces anxiety

---

### Returning User Welcome

**Pattern**: Welcome back + personalization

**Examples**:
- "Welcome back, Sarah!"
- "Good morning! Here's what you missed."
- "You're back! Let's pick up where you left off."

**Why it works**:
- Personal and warm
- Contextual to user's activity

**Avoid**:
- Showing "Welcome back" to first-time users (check user state)

---

### Value-Focused Welcome

**Pattern**: Welcome + what they'll accomplish

**Examples**:
- "Welcome! Let's build your first project together."
- "Hi! We'll help you create your profile and start connecting with your team."
- "Welcome aboard! Let's get you set up so you can start tracking your goals."

**Why it works**:
- Outcome-focused
- Shows immediate value
- Motivating

---

## Onboarding Steps & Progress

### Step Titles

**Pattern**: Action-oriented titles that describe what the user will accomplish

**Examples**:
- "Create your profile"
- "Invite your team"
- "Set your goals"
- "Choose your plan"
- "Connect your tools"

**Why it works**:
- Clear about what happens in this step
- Action-oriented (verbs)

---

### Step Descriptions

**Pattern**: Brief explanation (1-2 sentences) of why this step matters

**Examples**:

**Profile setup**:
- "Add your photo and bio so your team can recognize you."
- "Tell us a bit about yourself. This helps personalize your experience."

**Team invitations**:
- "Invite your teammates to collaborate on projects."
- "Work is better together. Add your team to get started."

**Tool connections**:
- "Connect your favorite tools to sync your work automatically."
- "Link your accounts to import your existing data."

**Why it works**:
- Explains the "why" not just the "what"
- Shows value of completing the step

---

### Progress Indicators

**Pattern**: Show where they are in the process

**Text indicators**:
- "Step 1 of 4"
- "2 of 5 steps complete"
- "Almost done! One more step."

**Progress labels**:
- "Getting started" (Step 1)
- "Personalize" (Step 2)
- "Invite team" (Step 3)
- "You're all set!" (Complete)

**Why it works**:
- Manages expectations
- Shows progress
- Motivates completion

---

### Skip Options

**Pattern**: Allow users to skip optional steps gracefully

**Examples**:
- "Skip for now"
- "Skip this step"
- "Do this later"
- "I'll add this later"
- "Remind me later"

**Why it works**:
- Respects user's time
- Reduces friction
- "For now" implies they can return

**Avoid**:
- "Skip" alone (too abrupt)
- "No thanks" (feels negative)

---

## Tutorial Content

### Tutorial Introductions

**Pattern**: Brief overview of what they'll learn + time estimate

**Examples**:
- "This 3-minute tutorial will teach you the basics of creating projects."
- "Let's walk through your first task together. It'll only take a moment."
- "Quick tour: We'll show you the 5 most important features in under 2 minutes."

**Why it works**:
- Sets time expectations
- Shows value (what they'll learn)
- Optional but encouraging

---

### Tutorial Steps

**Keep each step focused on ONE concept**

**Structure**: [Title] + [Brief description] + [Action to take]

**Example**:
```
Step 1: Create a project

Projects help you organize your work by goal or team.

Click "New Project" in the top right.
```

**Why it works**:
- One idea per step
- Clear action to take
- Context before action

---

### Tutorial Navigation

**Button labels**:
- "Next"
- "Got it"
- "Show me"
- "Continue"
- "Finish tutorial"

**Progress indicators**:
- "1 of 5"
- "Next: Add team members"

**Exit options**:
- "Skip tutorial"
- "I'll explore on my own"
- "Exit tutorial"

**Why it works**:
- Clear navigation
- Shows progress
- Easy to exit

---

### Tooltips in Tutorials

**Pattern**: Point out specific features with brief explanations

**Examples**:
- "This is your dashboardâ€”you'll see all your projects here"
- "Click here to invite team members"
- "The + button creates new items"

**Why it works**:
- Contextual help
- Points to specific UI elements
- Brief and actionable

## Empty States (Onboarding Context)

### First-Time Empty States

**Pattern**: Explain why it's empty + encourage first action

**Examples**:

**No projects**:
- "You haven't created any projects yet. Click 'New Project' to get started."
- "No projects yet. Create your first project to begin organizing your work."

**No team members**:
- "You're the only member of this team. Invite your teammates to start collaborating."
- "Build your team! Click 'Invite' to add members."

**No files**:
- "No files uploaded yet. Drag and drop files here or click 'Upload'."
- "This folder is empty. Add your first file to get started."

**Why it works**:
- Explains the state
- Provides clear action
- Encouraging tone

---

### Feature Discovery

**Pattern**: Introduce new features when relevant

**Examples**:
- "New feature! You can now @mention teammates in comments."
- "Did you know? Press '/' to quickly search anything."
- "Tip: You can drag tasks to reorder them."

**Why it works**:
- Contextual (shown when relevant)
- Brief and non-intrusive
- Highlights value

---

## Help & Guidance

### Inline Help Text

**Pattern**: Brief guidance below form fields or UI elements

**Examples**:

**Password field**:
- "At least 8 characters and include a number"

**Privacy setting**:
- "Only you can see private projects"

**File upload**:
- "Maximum file size: 10 MB. Supported formats: PDF, JPG, PNG"

**Why it works**:
- Proactive help (before errors occur)
- Contextual
- Brief and scannable

---

### Tooltips

**Examples**:

**Icon explanations**:
- "Verified badge confirms your identity to other users"
- "Lock icon means this project is private"
- "Star to add to favorites"

**Feature explanations**:

**Status indicators**:
- "Last saved 2 minutes ago"
- "Syncing with cloud..."
- "Draftâ€”not visible to others yet"

**Why it works**:
- Brief and scannable
- Provides context without cluttering UI
- Explains "why" not just "what"

## Success Moments

### Completion Messages

**Pattern**: Celebrate + summarize what was accomplished + next steps

**Examples**:

**Onboarding complete**:
- "You're all set! You've created your profile, invited your team, and set up your first project. Ready to get started?"

**First task completed**:
- "Nice work! You've completed your first task. Keep the momentum going."

**Tutorial finished**:
- "Tutorial complete! You now know how to create projects, invite members, and organize tasks. Start building your first project."

**Why it works**:
- Celebrates achievement
- Summarizes learning
- Encourages next action

---

### Encouragement Along the Way

**Pattern**: Positive reinforcement during onboarding

**Examples**:
- "Great! One more step."
- "Almost there!"
- "Perfect! You're doing great."
- "Nice choice!"
- "Looking good!"

**Why it works**:
- Motivating
- Reduces anxiety
- Makes experience feel supportive

**Don't overdo it**:
- Use sparingly
- Genuine enthusiasm only
- Avoid being patronizing

## Opt-In Requests

### Email Collection

**Pattern**: Explain value + promise privacy

**Examples**:
- "Enter your email to save your work. We'll never spam you."
- "Get notified when someone shares a file with you. You can unsubscribe anytime."
- "We'll use this to send you important account updates. No marketing emails."

**Why it works**:
- Clear about why email is needed
- Addresses privacy concerns
- Sets expectations

---

### Notification Permission

**Pattern**: Specific benefit + reassurance

**Examples**:
- "Get notified when someone mentions you in a comment. You can change this anytime in settings."
- "We'll alert you when tasks are due. You can customize which notifications you receive."
- "Enable notifications to stay updated on your team's activity."

**Why it works**:
- Specific about what notifications do
- Shows it's configurable
- Clear value

---

### Location Permission

**Pattern**: Explain why + what it's used for

**Examples**:
- "We'll use your location to show nearby stores and events."
- "Allow location access to find the closest delivery option."
- "Location helps us provide accurate local weather in your dashboard."

**Why it works**:
- Transparent about usage
- Clear benefit
- Respects privacy

---

## Progressive Disclosure

### Feature Introduction

**Pattern**: Introduce features when users need them, not all at once

**Examples**:

**After creating first project**:
- "Want to invite teammates? Click 'Share' to add collaborators."

**After adding several tasks**:
- "Pro tip: Use labels to organize tasks by category."

**After completing tasks**:
- "You can view completed tasks in the 'Done' tab."

**Why it works**:
- Not overwhelming
- Contextual to user's progress
- Incremental learning

---

### Advanced Features

**Pattern**: Wait until users are comfortable before showing advanced features

**Examples**:
- "You're a pro! Ready to try advanced filters?"
- "Now that you've mastered the basics, check out keyboard shortcuts."
- "Level up: Try these advanced features to save time."

**Why it works**:
- Respects learning curve
- Feels like unlocking new abilities
- Empowering, not overwhelming

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**Don't**:
- Show too many steps at once (overwhelming)
- Use vague labels like "Setup" or "Configuration"
- Force users through every step (allow skipping)
- Use technical jargon in onboarding
- Congratulate users excessively ("Amazing! Incredible! Fantastic!")
- Hide progress (users want to know how long it takes)
- Make onboarding too long (aim for under 5 minutes)
- Repeat information users already provided

---

## Onboarding Copy Checklist

Before shipping onboarding:

- [ ] Does it welcome users warmly?
- [ ] Does it set clear expectations (time, steps)?
- [ ] Is each step focused on one concept?
- [ ] Does it explain "why" not just "what"?
- [ ] Can users skip optional steps?
- [ ] Is progress clearly indicated?
- [ ] Does it celebrate completion?
- [ ] Are next steps clear after onboarding?
- [ ] Is it under 5 minutes to complete?
- [ ] Have you tested it with new users?

---

## Summary

**Great onboarding copy**:
- Welcomes users warmly
- Sets clear expectations
- Focuses on value and outcomes
- Shows progress
- Allows flexibility (skipping)
- Celebrates small wins
- Guides to first success

**The goal**: Get users to their first "aha moment" as quickly as possible while making them feel supported and confident.
